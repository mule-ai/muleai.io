# External Topic Research Findings

## Selected Topic: Golang AI Agent Frameworks in 2026

### Why This Topic?
- Directly relevant to Mule's focus on AI development in Go
- Growing ecosystem of AI agent frameworks in Go
- Interesting to the Mule AI community who use Go
- Provides value beyond just a link - covers the landscape and trade-offs

### Key Frameworks Covered:

1. **Google ADK (Agent Development Kit)**
   - Best for: Enterprise multi-agent systems
   - Supports: Google AI models (Gemini), Native A2A protocol
   - Hosting: Google Cloud optimized
   - Difficulty: Advanced

2. **Firebase Genkit**
   - Best for: Rapid prototyping & RAG apps
   - Supports: Gemini, Gemma, Multi-provider
   - Hosting: Firebase, GCP
   - Difficulty: Beginner

3. **LangChainGo**
   - Best for: LLM app development
   - Supports: 10+ providers (OpenAI, Anthropic, AWS, Ollama, etc.)
   - Difficulty: Intermediate

4. **Eino** (cloudwego)
   - Best for: High-scale production
   - Draws from LangChain, Google ADK, designed to follow Golang conventions
   - Provides: ChatModel, Tool, Retriever, ChatTemplate components
   - Agent Development Kit with tool use, multi-agent coordination
   - Intermediate difficulty

5. **Jetify AI SDK**
   - Best for: Multi-provider abstraction
   - Difficulty: Beginner

6. **Anyi**
   - Best for: Workflow automation & RPA
   - Difficulty: Intermediate

### Key Insight:
Python usually gets all the attention in AI, but Golang is far from out of the game. For developers who care about speed and reliability, Go is a go-to choice for building AI agents. Go's native concurrency, strong typing, and REST/RPC support make it ideal for production-grade AI solutions.
